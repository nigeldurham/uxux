sunday

mobile gallery

connecting local artists with global buyers

problem

artists need to connect with the international art market

solution

using traveling curators as our bridge, we can bring artists and buyers together by leveraging social influence

ideation

our goal is to help local art sellers reach out to the global art market; how might we design an interface that best presents artist work to a global fine-art market?

wireframing

having designed a flow for artists and buyers alike, artists are able to share their work locally or globally, while buyers are able to search for nearby or global artists. buyers can approach artists via a messaging system accessed through the creator's personal portfolio

prototyping

artists are encouraged to upload media using their device camera, wherein they can include details about their work and choose where to share on the platform. buyers are able to view shared work, learn about creators, and contact artists through the app

usability testing

artist were dismayed by not being able to organize work within or across projects, while curators noted they were seeking more detailed information about artist work, within or across projects

feature prioritization

researching archival features found on online galleries or artist portfolios, an archive system was designed for uploaded media covering most common archive features for fine artwork

final prototype

implementing structural changes in response to users' primary pain-points, the final product contains detailed information architecture inspired by fine art archiving

product

a dynamic mobile arts marketplace for nearby or global networks that incorporates flexible information architecture for dynamic archives of artist work in visually minimal personal portfolios
