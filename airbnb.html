with the goal of connecting events with event spaces, we designed a platform for users seeking to throw events to easily find spaces to host their events

design goals

we aimed to thread two distinct user flows seamlessly within the existing airbnb experience, employing the canonical design process that ranges from low-fidelity wireframing to high-fidelity prototyping

process

working alongside 4 other designers, i developed the intial design system and branding for this experience. we shared all roles for this product including iterating, testing, and stakeholder presentation

user journey

bringing together our two personas, space-seller and event-thrower, we designed an intuitive platform where both parties could browse or search for available events or spaces

research

user research and contextual inquiry indicated this would be a meaningful product for native or platform-na√Øve users - beginning with user expectations, we developed a prototype for the platform

prototyping

for low-fidelity wireframes, venue owners are able to create a profile for their venue, include relevant venue information, and share openly on the platform. event hosts are similarly able to profile their event, include event details, and share publicly on platform. both parties are able to reach out to one another directly

usability testing

usability testing allowed us to identify user pain points, including issues with the general layout, some confusion about specific CTAs during task flows, and event tag-language that was uncommon or unhelpful

priortizing

ample user testing and user interviews, along with stakeholder considerations, funneled our core feature modifications into a more cohesive product solution

final prototype

a functional user experience running on invision. usability testing confirmed that the flow was indistinguishable from the current airbnb application
